Indian Festivals

Discover the colorful festivals that showcase India's rich culture and traditions.

Diwali (Festival of Lights)

Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is celebrated with great enthusiasm across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. People light oil lamps, burst firecrackers, and exchange sweets, creating a vibrant and joyous atmosphere.

Holi (Festival of Colors)

Holi, celebrated in March, is the festival of colors and marks the arrival of spring. People smear each other with colors, dance to music, and enjoy traditional sweets like **gujiya**. It is a celebration of joy, love, and the triumph of good over evil.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is a 10-day festival celebrating the birth of Lord Ganesha, the god of wisdom and prosperity. People install beautifully crafted idols of Ganesha in homes and public places. Devotees offer prayers, perform rituals, and immerse the idols in water on the final day, symbolizing his return to Mount Kailash.

Onam

Onam is the harvest festival celebrated with great deal in Kerala. It commemorates the homecoming of the legendary king Mahabali. The festival is marked by vibrant floral decorations (Pookalam), traditional boat races (Vallam Kali), and a grand feast known as Onam Sadhya. It showcases Kerala's culture, unity, and traditions.
