Explore Romania's foods

Join us on a journey to experience fascinating foods with romanian cuisine.

1. Mămăligă

Mămăligă is a classic Romanian dish made from cornmeal, similar to polenta. Simple yet versatile, it's cooked to a creamy consistency and often served as a side to stews, grilled meats, or cheese. Traditionally paired with sour cream and brined cheese, mămăligă is a comforting staple in Romanian cuisine.

2. Mici

Mici are a type of grilled sausage made from a mix of beef, lamb, and pork, seasoned with garlic, thyme, and other spices. These skinless sausages are a favorite at barbecues and are traditionally served with mustard and bread.

3. Sarmale

Sarmale are cabbage rolls stuffed with a mix of minced meat (usually pork) and rice, seasoned with herbs. These rolls are cooked slowly in a tomato sauce and are a popular dish for holidays and celebrations.

4. Cozonac

Cozonac is a traditional Romanian sweet bread, typically made for holidays like Christmas and Easter. It is filled with ingredients such as walnuts, cocoa, or Turkish delight, giving it a rich and festive flavor.
