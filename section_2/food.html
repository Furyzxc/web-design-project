The Food of Ireland

Food and recipes that orginated from Ireland.

1. Irish Soda Brown Bread

Brown Bread sitting in a basket alongside Irish butter.

Traditional Irish bread was pan-baked on a griddle as the domestic flour of Ireland did not have the necessary properties to rise when combined with yeast.
Baking soda became the appropriate alternative, with many varieties such as being baked with Guinness, stout, or walnuts.

2. Irish Stew

Bowl of Irish stew that includes potatoes, carrots and meat.

Old Irish recipes are scarce, due to poor documentation, but the first record of this recipe dates back to the 1600s, from the O'Brein family.
"To make an Irish stew of mutton Season the bones of a neck of mutton with pepper and salt, put it down with a layer of onions, put them in covered stewpan, to keep in the steam & as much water as will cover it. The chops must be very tender, but as they are all put down together, the potatoes must be taken out first, as they burst".

3. Coddle

A bowl of Irish coddle, consisting of potatoes, sliced sausage and carrots.

Coddle is an Irish dish that is made up of mostly leftovers, and was popular in Dublin.
Commonly consists of layers of sliced pork sausages and rashers with chunky potatoes, sliced onion, salt, pepper and herbs.
The dish is braised in stock produced by boiling parts of bacon and sausage, and the entire is cooked in a pot.

4. Bacon and Cabbage

A bowl of Irish coddle, consisting of potatoes, sliced sausage and carrots.

Bacon and cabbage is a traditional dish associated with Ireland and England.
The dish consisted of sliced bacon boiled with cabbage and potatoes, and is served with some of the boiled juices and a white sauce that consists of milk, flour, butter and parsley.

History

Bacon and cabbage was common in Ireland and Britain as the ingredients were usually grown by the family, aswell as reared animals.
